This can't go on

People with MND are being forced to wait to receive the care and support they desperately need through local authorities.

Too often, people are dying before statutory services are put in place. It’s truly shocking. The challenge Scotland’s care system faces is massive – but it’s not insurmountable. The reality is, many of the issues people with MND face could be overcome if we simply had a system which recognised the speed at which MND can progress.

What we're up against

MND Scotland’s Advocacy service was set up to guide people through a labyrinth of confusing processes; and to be there when the system fails. Every year we see the same problems again and again.

Social care

Many are forced to wait weeks for an initial care package to be put in place; even longer for it to be increased; and longer still (if at all) to secure overnight or 24 hour care.

Home adaptations

Delays in receiving adaptations can leave people with MND effectively trapped in their houses, as simple tasks like sitting, standing and washing become harder by the day.

Accessible housing

Despite the fact average life expectancy is just 18 months from diagnosis, many local authorities place people with MND on frustrating slow waiting lists.

Our message to the next Scottish Government

Right now, our Campaigns Team are meeting with researchers and candidates from all parties, laying bear the problems cause by the slow speed of the system.

Our message is simple: People with MND deserve care and support which can keep pace with MND. This demands fast-tracking and increased capacity, delivered through:

  1. A National Care Service for Scotland
  2. A National Accessible Housing Strategy
